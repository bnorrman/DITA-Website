Types of Volunteers

There are many different ways that you can volunteer for the Advocates for Human Rights. Below are a few of the types of volunteers we need.

Asylum Attorneys

Asylum attorneys represent asylum-seekers during all stages of the legal process. You need to be admitted to a bar association in any state to be an asylum attorney, but you do not need specialized knowledge about immigration or asylum.

Bond Representation Attorneys

Bond representation attorneys represent detained immigrants during their removal proceedings. You need a license to practice law in a U.S. jurisdiction to be a bond representation attorney, but you do not need specialized knowledge about immigration.

Immigration Court Observers

Immigration court observers attend immigration court hearings and report on issues of concern, including access to legal counsel, family support, the manner of arrest, and the ability of the detained to argue their deportation. You do not need specialized immigration knowledge or a legal license to be an immigration court observer.

Interns

Interns can take many different roles, including writing web content, researching country conditions, and performing client intake, among others. We expect interns to commit 20-40 hours per week during the summer or 8-12 hours per week during the rest of the year.

Interpretors and Translators

Intepretors and translators translate documents and assist during in-person meetings with clients. We particularly need volunteers who speak French, Amharic, Oromo, Somali, Arabic, Spanish, and Hmong.

Office and Events Support

Office and events supporters help with common tasks at the downtown Minneapolis office and at events. This includes administrative functions, greeting guests, and photographing events.

Remote Database Volunteers

Remote database volunteers keep track of deadlines for engaging with international human rights organizations. Each volunteer will be assigned 1-3 organizations to watch and will submit weekly reports with any new deadlines.

Research and Documentation

Research and documentation volunteers perform legal research and fact-finding on human rights issues at the local, national, and international level. Common tasks include legal research, writing, and editing.
